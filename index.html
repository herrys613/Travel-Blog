Wandering Through the Colors of Morocco

By Hershy · Febuary 2023

Thursday - Day 1

We landed at Marrakesh (RAK) airport Thursday night. We took a 15 minutetaxi to our hotel the Tempoo Hôtel Marrakech, After arriving, we ate some food we had brought along for supper and went straight to bed.

Friday - Day 2

We woke up Friday morning and started the day with a 40-minute walk to the old city (the Medina). While wandering through the narrow streets and local markets, we stopped at Tinsmiths Square – a small but lively spot where local guys were busy hammering away at copper and metal, making all kinds of cool traditional pieces right there in front of us.

From there, we headed to the Slat Al Azama Synagogue (10 DH entry fee), a historic Sephardic synagogue built in 1942, it showcases traditional Moroccan synagogue architecture with a central courtyard. Just a short walk away is the Marrakech Jewish Cemetery, (entrance is free, though a small donation to the cemetery guard is expected -about 10-20 DH per person are appropriate).

After that, we walked over to the El Badi Palace (100 DH entry fee), once a lavish 16th-century palace, now a grand set of ruins. Despite its current state, the scale of the walls and the layout still give you a sense of how impressive it once was.

From there, we continued to the famous Jemaa el-Fnaa Square — full of energy, street performers, market stalls, snake charmers, fresh orange juice vendors, and the buzz of Marrakech life.

We took a taxi back to the hotel. Later in the afternoon, we walked about 10 minutes to Mövenpick Hotel Mansour Eddahbi, where we’d be having our Shabbas meals — just to check out the route ahead of time.

Shabbas - Day 3

Friday night and Shabbat morning, we davened and ate meals at the Mövenpick Hotel Mansour Eddahbi — a beautiful hotel!, they served traditional moroccan food.

In the afternoon, we took a 20-minute walk to Temple Beth El Synagogue for Mincha.

Motzei Shabbos we went out to eat at Al Dente, a kosher Italian restaurant. (Right across the street is Wasabi, a kosher sushi place)

Sunday - Day 4

We woke up super early around 5:00 AM and started our journey toward the Merzouga Desert, which is part of the stunning Sahara Dessert. it's a 9 hour drive.

We made a stop at Atlas Studios in Ouarzazate — a famous film studio where movies like Gladiator and The Mummy were shot. ussaly a 3.5 hour drive from Marrakesh but due to a snowfall in the High Atlas Mountains (about a foot of snow, which is very rare in Morocco), the roads were closed.

So we had to reroute all the way down through Agadir, which added about 4 more hours to the drive.

Eventually, we reached the edge of the Merzouga Desert, where the camp had arranged a meeting point. From there, they took us in a jeep — about a 15-minute drive into the dunes — to our site, SIROCCO Luxury Camp. a beautiful glamping site in the middle of the desert.

We settled in, ate super that we bought along, and spent the night in a beautiful desert tent surrounded by sand dunes.

Monday - Day 5

We woke up in the desert and started the day with a short camel ride through the dunes, After that, a jeep took us back out of the desert where we had scheduled an ATV tour.

Our driver picked us up from there and we headed to Toulal, where we visited the grave of Reb Yitzhak Abuhatzeira.

From there, we continued on to Fes — about a 5-hour drive. We were originally planning to stop at the Cèdre Gouraud Forest, known for its ancient cedar trees and wild monkeys, but we ran out of time.

We arrived in Fes in the evening and went straight to eat at Dragon Kosher Restaurant, Then we checked in and went to sleep at Hôtel Royal Urban Concept.

Tuesday - Day 6

In the morning, we visited Art D’ARGILE, a local ceramic workshop where we got to see the process of traditional Moroccan pottery and mosaic-making —was very intresting and super impressive.

After that, we went to the Jewish Cemetery of Fes, From there, we went into the Shuk (market) and stopped at the Aben Danan Synagogue, located right in the heart of the shuk.

Across from the Shuk, we visited the beautiful Royal Palace of Fes, known for its huge golden doors (can’t go inside, but even just standing outside is amazing).

We continued to the famous Tanneries of Fes, where leather is still dyed by hand in colorful vats — a must-see.

In the afternoon, we picked up dinner from Dragon Kosher Restaurant,

We then Drove 3 hour and 45-minute drive to Chefchaouen, the "Blue City." We stopped at a beautiful lookout spot with an amazing view of the blue-painted town.

we walked aroun the city and we drove 1.5 hours to the grave of Rabbi Amram ben Diwan, where we also ate dinner and stayed overnight.

Wednesday - Day 7

After breakfast, we drove about 3.5 hours to Casablanca, First up was Mohammed V Square, a large plaza with fountains and flocks of birds. For a small fee, local vendors gave us bird food — and the birds fly right into our hands.

Next, we visited Beth-El Temple, a beautiful synagogue in the city. We took a short walk from there and had lunch at AROMA Kosher Food & Bakery, After lunch, we made our way to the Hassan II Mosque, one of the largest mosques in the world.

Afterward, we stopped at the Morocco Mall, a massive shopping center with everything from luxury brands to souvenirs.

In the afternoon, we explored the Medina Market. When we arrived a local man approached us and offered to take us to the house of Rav Chaim Pinto. We decided to go, and when we got there, we met some of his grandchildren who gave us blessings. They even let us wear Rav Chaim Pinto’s bekitche.

We ended the day by visiting the cemetery where Rav Chaim Pinto is buried, then had dinner at Le Chandelier Kosher Restaurant, and spent our final night at the Swiss Hotel.

Thursday - Day 8

We woke up and headed to Casablanca Airport (CMN) for our flight back home.
